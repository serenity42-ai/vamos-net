# Padel Glossary: 80+ Terms Every Player Should Know

Padel speaks Spanish. Most of the vocabulary you'll hear on court — and most of the shots that have actual names — comes straight from the Argentine and Spanish circuits where the sport grew up. Here are the terms, alphabetised, that you'll meet within your first month of playing or watching.

## A

**Ace**
A serve the receiving pair fails to touch, winning the point outright. Less common in padel than tennis because the underhand serve produces less speed, but a well-placed serve to the body or into the corner can still produce one.

**Advantage (Ventaja)**
When the score reaches deuce (40–40) under traditional scoring, the pair that wins the next point has the advantage and must win the following point to take the game. Professional padel typically uses the golden point rule instead.

**Approach Shot**
A shot played while moving toward the net, intended to transition from defending at the back to attacking at the front.

## B

**Bajada**
A powerful overhead smash played off the ball after it has bounced off the back glass wall. The ball rises high after hitting the wall; the player attacks it on the way down. One of the most spectacular shots in padel.

**Bandeja**
A controlled overhead played from a high position, typically with slice or side spin. Unlike a full smash, the bandeja is a tactical shot designed to maintain net position rather than win the point outright. Played with a continental grip and a sideways body position. Arguably the most important shot in intermediate and advanced padel.

**Bolea**
A volley played with an overhead motion, similar to a bandeja but hit closer to the body and with a flatter trajectory. Used when the ball is not high enough for a full bandeja or smash.

**Break**
Winning a game when the opposing pair is serving. Significant in padel because the serving pair typically has a slight advantage.

## C

**Cajón (Cajita)**
Literally "box" or "little box." Playing the ball into the corner where the side wall meets the back wall, creating a difficult bounce for the opponent.

**Chiquita**
A soft, low shot played from behind the service line toward the feet of the opponents at the net. The chiquita is one of padel's most important tactical shots, designed to force the net players to hit the ball upward and create an attacking opportunity for the pair at the back. The name comes from the Spanish for "small."

**Contrapared**
Playing the ball off your own back wall. When a shot from the opponents bounces on your side and then hits the back glass, you can let it rebound and play it after it comes off the wall. A fundamental defensive technique.

**Contraataque**
A counterattack. Playing an aggressive shot from a defensive position, typically from the back of the court.

**Cross-court**
A shot played diagonally from one side of the court to the other. A staple of padel strategy, particularly when creating angles.

## D

**Dead Ball (Bola Muerta)**
A ball played with minimal pace that drops just over the net with heavy backspin, making it hard for the opponents to attack. Also refers to any ball that has stopped bouncing.

**Dejada**
A drop shot. A soft, delicate shot played to land just over the net with minimal bounce, designed to catch opponents off guard when they are positioned at the back of the court.

**Deuce**
40–40. Under the golden point rule used in professional padel, a single deciding point is played. Under the advantage system, a pair must win two consecutive points from deuce.

**Doble Pared (Double Wall)**
A shot that hits two walls (typically side then back, or vice versa) before bouncing on the court. Creates unpredictable bounces and is hard to return.

**Drive**
A flat or slightly topspin groundstroke played from the baseline area, similar to a tennis drive but with less power and more emphasis on placement.

## F

**Falta (Fault)**
An illegal serve. A fault occurs when the serve does not land in the correct service box, when the server hits the ball above waist height, or when the ball hits the net and then contacts a wall before bouncing in the service box.

**Flat (Plano)**
A shot hit without significant spin, on a relatively straight trajectory.

**Fondo de Pista**
The back of the court, near the back glass wall. Players positioned there are typically defending.

## G

**Globo (Lob)**
A high, deep shot aimed over the heads of the opponents at the net, intended to push them back to the baseline. One of the most important defensive weapons in padel. A well-executed lob with topspin is hard to attack and is the primary way to regain the net position from defence.

**Golden Point (Punto de Oro)**
The rule used in professional padel where, at deuce, a single deciding point determines the game. The receiving pair chooses which side to receive on.

## H

**Huevo**
Literally "egg." A very fast, hard shot aimed directly at the opponent's body, making it extremely difficult to return.

## J

**Juego**
Game. The completion of a scoring sequence (15, 30, 40, game).

## K

**Kick**
A shot played with heavy topspin that bounces high off the surface or wall, forcing the opponent to play the ball at an uncomfortable height.

## L

**Let**
A serve that clips the net cord and lands in the correct service box. The serve is replayed.

**Liftado**
A shot with significant topspin. Used to create high bounces and to make sure the ball dips over the net.

**Lob (Globo)**
See Globo.

## M

**Match Ball**
A point that, if won by the leading pair, ends the match.

**Match Point**
The state of play when one pair is one point away from winning the match.

**Muro (Wall)**
Any of the enclosing walls of the padel court. Also used colloquially to describe a player who returns everything, acting as a human wall.

## N

**Net (Red)**
The barrier dividing the court, 88 cm at the centre and 92 cm at the posts.

**No Man's Land**
The area between the service line and the net where players are vulnerable to shots at their feet. In padel, you should generally be either at the net or at the back wall, not in this transitional zone.

## O

**Out (Fuera)**
A ball that leaves the court over the walls or fencing without bouncing on the opponents' side first. Also a ball that hits the mesh on the full (without bouncing first).

## P

**Pala**
The padel racket. Unlike a tennis racket, a pala has no strings. Solid hitting surface made of carbon fiber, fiberglass, or composite materials, with a foam or EVA rubber core. Sometimes called a "raqueta."

**Pared (Wall)**
The walls of the court. *Jugar la pared* means to play the ball off the wall.

**Par (Pair)**
A padel team. Padel is doubles only, so every team is a pair.

**Playa**
Literally "beach." A point where the ball rolls along the ground after the second bounce, indicating the point is over.

**Por Cuatro (By Four)**
A ball that exits the court over the 4-metre back wall. When this happens off a smash, the point is not automatically won; the defending pair can run outside the court and try to return the ball. One of padel's signature scenarios.

**Por Tres (By Three)**
A ball that exits the court over the 3-metre section of the side wall. As with por cuatro, the defending pair can attempt to play the ball from outside the court.

**Punto de Oro**
See Golden Point.

## R

**Raqueta**
See Pala.

**Rebote**
The rebound of the ball off any wall. Reading rebounds off glass, concrete, and different wall angles is fundamental to padel.

**Red**
The net.

**Rematador**
A player who specializes in smashes and overhead attacks. Typically the more aggressive player in a pair.

**Remate**
A smash or overhead attacking shot. The most powerful shot in padel, played with full overhead motion to drive the ball down into the opponents' court.

**Revés**
Backhand. The shot played on the non-dominant side.

**Rulo**
A shot played with extreme side spin, causing the ball to curve and bounce unpredictably off the walls.

## S

**Saque (Serve)**
The underhand serve that begins each point. The server must bounce the ball and strike it at or below waist height.

**Set**
Won by the first pair to reach six games with a two-game lead, or by winning a tiebreak at 6–6.

**Slice**
A shot played with backspin, keeping the ball low after the bounce. Used on bandejas, volleys, and defensive returns.

**Smash**
See Remate.

**Split Step**
A small, balanced jump just before the opponent hits the ball, letting you react in any direction. Fundamental footwork in padel.

## T

**Tiebreak (Muerte Súbita)**
Decides a set when the score reaches 6–6 in games. First to 7 points with a 2-point lead.

**Topspin**
Forward rotation applied to the ball, making it dip downward and bounce higher than a flat shot.

**Tres Metros**
Literally "three metres." The 3-metre-high section of the side wall. Shots that clear this section let opponents play from outside the court.

**Tuya (Yours)**
Called to indicate the ball is your partner's. Clear communication between partners is essential.

## V

**Ventaja**
See Advantage.

**Víbora (Viper)**
An advanced overhead similar to the bandeja but played with more aggressive side spin and a sharper downward angle. Hit with a pronation of the wrist; the trajectory sends the ball into the side wall and then away from the opponent. One of the most effective attacking shots from the net position and a hallmark of advanced play.

**Volea (Volley)**
A shot played before the ball bounces. Volleying is central in padel; pairs aim to control the net position throughout the point.

## W

**Winner**
A shot that wins the point outright without the opponent touching it.

**WPT**
World Padel Tour, the former professional circuit that was the dominant tour before the establishment of Premier Padel.

## X

**X4 (Por Cuatro)**
Shorthand for a ball exiting over the 4-metre back wall. See Por Cuatro.

## Tactical terms

**Australian Formation**
A positioning strategy where the serving pair starts with both players on the same side of the court, with the net player on the same side as the server rather than the opposite side.

**Cambio de Pared (Wall Change)**
A shot that redirects the ball from one wall to another, creating a change of direction that disrupts the opponents' positioning.

**Cruce (Cross)**
When the net player moves across to intercept a shot intended for their partner, cutting off the cross-court angle.

**Dos Contra Uno (Two vs One)**
A tactical pattern where both players target the weaker opponent, concentrating shots on one player to create opportunities.

**Posición de Red (Net Position)**
The dominant offensive position in padel, where both players of a pair stand close to the net and volley to control the point from there.

**Subida a Red (Approach to Net)**
Moving forward from the back of the court to the net, typically following a well-placed shot that puts the opponents under pressure.

---

You don't need every term on day one. Five will get you through your first match: **bandeja, víbora, chiquita, globo, bajada**. The rest will arrive as you play and watch.
