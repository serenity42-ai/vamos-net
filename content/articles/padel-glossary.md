# Padel Glossary: 80+ Terms Every Player Should Know

Padel has its own rich vocabulary, blending Spanish terminology with universal racket sport language. Whether you are watching your first Premier Padel match, joining a local league, or trying to understand what your partner means when they shout "tuya," this comprehensive glossary has you covered.

Terms are organized alphabetically for easy reference.

## A

**Ace**
A serve that the receiving pair fails to touch, winning the point outright. Aces are less common in padel than tennis because the underhand serve produces less speed, but a well-placed serve to the body or into the corner can still produce an ace.

**Advantage (Ventaja)**
When the score reaches deuce (40-40) under the traditional scoring system, the pair that wins the next point has the advantage. They must win the following point to take the game. In professional padel, the golden point rule often replaces this system.

**Approach Shot**
A shot played while moving toward the net, intended to transition from a defensive position at the back of the court to an offensive position at the net.

## B

**Bajada**
A powerful overhead smash played off the ball after it has bounced off the back glass wall. The ball rises high after hitting the wall, and the player attacks it aggressively on the way down. One of the most spectacular shots in padel.

**Bandeja**
A controlled overhead shot played from a high position, typically with slice or side spin. Unlike a full smash, the bandeja is a tactical shot designed to maintain the net position rather than win the point outright. It is played with a continental grip and a sideways body position. The bandeja is arguably the most important shot in intermediate and advanced padel.

**Bolea**
A volley played with an overhead motion, similar to a bandeja but hit closer to the body and typically with a flatter trajectory. Used when the ball is not high enough for a full bandeja or smash.

**Break**
Winning a game when the opposing pair is serving. Breaking serve is significant in padel because the serving pair typically has a slight advantage.

## C

**Cajon (Cajita)**
Literally "box" or "little box." Refers to playing the ball into the corner where the side wall meets the back wall, creating a difficult bounce for the opponent to handle.

**Chiquita**
A soft, low shot played from behind the service line toward the feet of the opponents at the net. The chiquita is one of padel's most important tactical shots, designed to force the net players to hit the ball upward, creating an opportunity for the pair at the back to attack. The name comes from the Spanish word for "small."

**Contrapared**
Playing the ball off your own back wall. When a shot from the opponents bounces on your side and then hits the back glass, you can let it rebound and play it after it comes off the wall. This is a fundamental defensive technique in padel.

**Contraataque**
A counterattack. Playing an aggressive shot from a defensive position, typically from the back of the court.

**Cross-court**
A shot played diagonally from one side of the court to the other. Cross-court shots are a staple of padel strategy, particularly when trying to create angles.

## D

**Dead Ball (Bola Muerta)**
A ball played with minimal pace that drops just over the net with heavy backspin, making it difficult for the opponents to attack. Also refers to any ball that has stopped bouncing.

**Dejada**
A drop shot. A soft, delicate shot played to land just over the net with minimal bounce, designed to catch the opponents off guard when they are positioned at the back of the court.

**Deuce**
When the score reaches 40-40 in a game. Under the golden point rule used in professional padel, a single deciding point is played. Under the advantage system, a pair must win two consecutive points from deuce.

**Doble Pared (Double Wall)**
A shot that hits two walls (typically the side wall and then the back wall, or vice versa) before bouncing on the court. These shots create unpredictable bounces and are difficult to return.

**Drive**
A flat or slightly topspin groundstroke played from the baseline area, similar to a tennis drive but typically with less power and more emphasis on placement.

## F

**Falta (Fault)**
An illegal serve. In padel, a fault occurs when the serve does not land in the correct service box, when the server hits the ball above waist height, or when the ball hits the net and then contacts a wall before bouncing in the service box.

**Flat (Plano)**
A shot hit without significant spin, traveling on a relatively straight trajectory.

**Fondo de Pista**
The back of the court, near the back glass wall. Players positioned at the fondo de pista are typically in a defensive position.

## G

**Globo (Lob)**
A high, deep shot aimed over the heads of the opponents at the net, intended to push them back to the baseline. The globo is one of the most important defensive weapons in padel. A well-executed lob with topspin can be very difficult to attack and is the primary way to regain the net position from a defensive stance.

**Golden Point (Punto de Oro)**
The rule used in professional padel where, at deuce (40-40), a single deciding point determines the winner of the game. The receiving pair chooses which side to receive on.

## H

**Huevo**
Literally "egg." A very fast, hard shot aimed directly at the opponent's body, making it extremely difficult to return. The name refers to the ball hitting the player (like getting "egged").

## J

**Juego**
Game. The completion of a scoring sequence (15, 30, 40, game).

## K

**Kick**
A shot played with heavy topspin that bounces high off the court surface or wall, forcing the opponent to play the ball at an uncomfortable height.

## L

**Let**
A serve that clips the net cord and lands in the correct service box. The serve is replayed with no penalty.

**Liftado**
A shot with significant topspin. Topspin shots are used to create high bounces and to ensure the ball dips over the net.

**Lob (Globo)**
See Globo. A high shot played over the opponents' heads.

## M

**Match Ball**
A point that, if won by the leading pair, ends the match.

**Match Point**
The state of play when one pair is one point away from winning the match.

**Muro (Wall)**
Refers to any of the enclosing walls of the padel court. Also used colloquially to describe a player who returns everything, acting as a human wall.

## N

**Net (Red)**
The barrier dividing the court, standing 88cm at the center and 92cm at the posts.

**No Man's Land**
The area between the service line and the net where players are vulnerable to shots at their feet. In padel, players should generally be either at the net or at the back wall, avoiding this transitional zone.

## O

**Out (Fuera)**
A ball that leaves the court over the walls or fencing without bouncing on the opponents' side first. Also applies to a ball that hits the fencing (mesh) on the full (without bouncing first).

## P

**Pala**
The padel racket. Unlike tennis rackets, a pala has no strings. It features a solid hitting surface made of carbon fiber, fiberglass, or composite materials, with a foam or EVA rubber core. Also sometimes called a "raqueta."

**Pared (Wall)**
The walls of the court. "Jugar la pared" means to play the ball off the wall.

**Par (Pair)**
A padel team. Padel is exclusively a doubles sport, so every team is a pair.

**Playa**
Literally "beach." Refers to a point where the ball rolls along the ground after the second bounce, indicating the point is over.

**Por Cuatro (By Four)**
A ball that exits the court by going over the back wall (4-meter wall). When this happens off a smash, the point is not automatically won -- the defending pair can attempt to run outside the court and return the ball. One of padel's most spectacular scenarios.

**Por Tres (By Three)**
A ball that exits the court by going over the side wall (3-meter section). As with por cuatro, the defending pair can attempt to play the ball from outside the court.

**Punto de Oro**
See Golden Point.

## R

**Raqueta**
See Pala. The padel racket.

**Rebote**
The rebound of the ball off any wall. Understanding how the ball rebounds off glass, concrete, and different wall angles is fundamental to padel.

**Red**
The net.

**Rematador**
A player who specializes in smashes and overhead attacks. Typically used to describe the more aggressive player in a pair.

**Remate**
A smash or overhead attacking shot. The most powerful shot in padel, played with full overhead motion to drive the ball down into the opponents' court.

**Revés**
Backhand. The shot played on the non-dominant side of the body.

**Rulo**
A shot played with extreme side spin, causing the ball to curve and bounce unpredictably off the walls.

## S

**Saque (Serve)**
The underhand serve that begins each point. The server must bounce the ball and strike it at or below waist height.

**Set**
A set is won by the first pair to reach six games with a two-game lead, or by winning a tiebreak at 6-6.

**Slice**
A shot played with backspin, causing the ball to stay low after bouncing. Slice is commonly used on bandejas, volleys, and defensive returns.

**Smash**
See Remate. A powerful overhead shot intended to win the point.

**Split Step**
A small, balanced jump performed just before the opponent hits the ball, allowing the player to react quickly in any direction. Fundamental footwork in padel.

## T

**Tiebreak (Muerte Subita)**
The procedure used to decide a set when the score reaches 6-6 in games. First to 7 points with a 2-point lead.

**Topspin**
Forward rotation applied to the ball, causing it to dip downward and bounce higher than a flat shot.

**Tres Metros**
Literally "three meters." Refers to the 3-meter-high section of the side wall. Shots that clear this section allow opponents to play from outside the court.

**Tuya (Yours)**
Called out to indicate that the ball is your partner's responsibility. Clear communication between partners is essential in padel.

## V

**Ventaja**
See Advantage.

**Vibora (Viper)**
An advanced overhead shot similar to the bandeja but played with more aggressive side spin and a sharper downward angle. The vibora is hit with a pronation of the wrist, creating a trajectory that sends the ball into the side wall and then bounces away from the opponent. It is one of the most effective attacking shots from the net position and a hallmark of advanced play.

**Volea (Volley)**
A shot played before the ball bounces on the ground. Volleying is a central skill in padel, as pairs aim to control the net position throughout the point.

## W

**Winner**
A shot that wins the point outright without the opponent being able to touch it.

**WPT**
World Padel Tour, the former professional circuit that was the dominant tour before the establishment of Premier Padel.

## X

**X4 (Por Cuatro)**
Shorthand for a ball exiting over the 4-meter back wall. See Por Cuatro.

## Tactical Terms

**Australian Formation**
A positioning strategy where the serving pair starts with both players on the same side of the court, with the net player positioned on the same side as the server rather than the opposite side.

**Cambio de Pared (Wall Change)**
A strategic shot aimed at redirecting the ball from one wall to another, creating a change of direction that disrupts the opponents' positioning.

**Cruce (Cross)**
When the net player moves across to intercept a shot intended for their partner, cutting off the cross-court angle.

**Dos Contra Uno (Two vs One)**
A tactical pattern where both players target the weaker opponent, concentrating shots on one player to create opportunities.

**Posicion de Red (Net Position)**
The dominant offensive position in padel, where both players of a pair stand close to the net, volleying and controlling the point from there.

**Subida a Red (Approach to Net)**
The act of moving forward from the back of the court to the net position, typically following a well-placed shot that puts the opponents under pressure.

## Wrapping Up

Padel's vocabulary reflects its Spanish-speaking roots and the tactical richness of the sport. You do not need to memorize every term to enjoy playing, but understanding the key shots -- bandeja, vibora, chiquita, globo, bajada -- and positional concepts will deepen your appreciation of the game and help you communicate more effectively with your partner on court.

Keep this glossary bookmarked. The more you play and watch, the more these terms will become second nature.
