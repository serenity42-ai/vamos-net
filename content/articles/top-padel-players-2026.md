# Top 10 Padel Players in 2026: Men's and Women's Rankings

Coello and Tapia opened 2026 the way they closed 2025: by winning everything in front of them. Behind them, the off-season tore the rest of the men's tour apart and put it back together in unfamiliar shapes. The women's side just witnessed the most consequential split in modern padel: Sánchez and Josemaría, one of the most successful pairs in the sport's history, dissolved.

Here are the top pairs in both draws as the 2026 season gets underway, based on the official FIP rankings and the start-of-season Premier Padel standings.

## Men's: the top pairs of 2026

### 1. Arturo Coello and Agustín Tapia (39,600 points)

The undisputed number ones, and it isn't close. The Spanish-Argentine duo accumulated 19,800 FIP ranking points each in 2025, winning 13 titles including the Premier Padel Finals. Their 47-match winning streak during the 2024 season remains one of the most extraordinary records in the sport.

**Arturo Coello** (born 2002, Spain) is the younger half and has developed into one of the most complete players in padel. His net game is devastating: fast hands, sharp reflexes, and an ability to close points that puts constant pressure on opponents. At 23, he's already at a level previous generations took years longer to reach.

**Agustín Tapia** (born 2000, Argentina) is widely regarded as the most naturally talented player in the game. His left-handed play from the backhand side complements Coello perfectly, and his shot-making borders on the absurd. Tapia's management of high-pressure moments has matured significantly, making an already exceptional player even more complete.

Three consecutive seasons of dominance (2023, 2024, 2025), no signs of slowing. They opened 2026 with a commanding win at the Riyadh P1, and the chasing pack has serious ground to cover.

### 2. Alejandro Galán and Federico Chingotto (34,640 points)

The only pair within realistic striking distance of the top. Their 2025 season produced 17,320 FIP points each, firmly establishing them as the second-best in the world.

**Alejandro Galán** (born 1996, Spain) is a former world number one (with Juan Lebrón) who reinvented his game alongside Chingotto. Power, particularly on the smash, and serious court coverage make him one of the most imposing physical presences in padel.

**Federico Chingotto** (born 1996, Argentina) brings flair, creativity, and some of the best defensive skills in the sport. His ability to retrieve seemingly impossible balls and turn defence into attack is central to the pair's identity. His theatrical style also makes him one of the most entertaining players on tour to watch.

### 3. Franco Stupaczuk and Miguel Yanguas (14,277 points)

One of the most talked-about partnerships of the off-season. Argentine grit plus Spanish tactical intelligence.

**Franco Stupaczuk** (born 1992, Argentina), known universally as Stupa, is a veteran who finished 2025 ranked fifth individually with 7,905 FIP points. Powerful left-handed game, years of top-level experience, reliability and firepower.

**Miguel Yanguas** (born 1998, Spain) finished 2025 ranked eighth individually as part of the Nieto/Yanguas pair, with 6,322 points. Technical skill and the ability to construct points from the right side complement Stupa's aggressive left-handed play.

### 4. Juan Lebrón and Leandro Augsburger (13,000 points)

Possibly the most intriguing partnership of 2026. A former world number one with one of the fastest-rising players of recent seasons.

**Juan Lebrón** (born 1995, Spain) needs no introduction. The former world number one (alongside Ale Galán from 2020 to 2023) finished 2025 ranked sixth individually with 7,845 points. After his partnership with Galán ended and subsequent pairings didn't recapture the magic, Lebrón has bet on a fresh start with Augsburger.

**Leandro Augsburger** (born 2001, Argentina) finished 2025 ranked tenth individually with 5,155 points and is widely considered one of the brightest young talents on tour. Aggressive style, comfort at the net. Could complement Lebrón's all-court game beautifully if the chemistry clicks.

### 5. Coki Nieto and Jon Sanz (11,447 points)

A new pairing born from the off-season reshuffle. Nieto (formerly with Yanguas) and Sanz bring solid technical foundations and the hunger of players seeking to establish themselves in the top tier.

### 6. Paquito Navarro and Francisco "Fran" Guerrero (10,525 points)

**Paquito Navarro** (born 1990, Spain) is one of the most charismatic and experienced players on tour. Powerful smash, crowd-pleasing style, still formidable. Paired with the consistent Guerrero, this team has the firepower to upset any pair on a given day.

### 7. Martín Di Nenno and Alejandro "Momo" González (9,460 points)

**Martín Di Nenno** (born 1995, Argentina) is a tactical mastermind whose cerebral approach to padel sets him apart. His partnership with González pairs his strategic brain with a solid right-side player. Di Nenno's ability to read the game and construct points makes this pair dangerous in extended rallies.

### 8. Javi Garrido and Lucas Bergamini (7,078 points)

Rounding out the top eight. Garrido and Bergamini represent the depth of talent on the 2026 tour. Both are capable of top-level padel, and their consistency could see them climb further as the season progresses.

## Women's: the top pairs of 2026

The women's game underwent its most dramatic off-season in years, headlined by the split of Ariana Sánchez and Paula Josemaría, one of the most successful partnerships in padel history. The ripple effects have reshaped the entire top 10.

### 1. Gemma Triay and Delfina Brea

The undisputed world number ones. Triay and Brea closed 2025 with 18,060 FIP points each, comfortably atop the rankings. Consistency, tactical versatility, and the ability to perform in the biggest moments have made them the benchmark pair in women's padel.

**Gemma Triay** (born 1993, Spain) is one of the most decorated players in the women's game. Her left-handed play, tactical intelligence, and fierce competitiveness define the pair's identity. Triay's decision to stay with Brea heading into 2026 — reportedly turning down an approach from Josemaría — confirmed her commitment to the project.

**Delfina Brea** (born 2000, Argentina) has matured into one of the most complete players in women's padel. Powerful groundstrokes from the right side, improving net skills, the perfect complement to Triay's all-court game. At 25, her ceiling is still enormous.

### 2. Beatriz González and Paula Josemaría

The biggest new partnership of 2026. After five years of dominance alongside Ariana Sánchez, **Paula Josemaría** (born 1996, Spain) — ranked third individually in 2025 with 14,720 points — has formed a new pair with **Beatriz González** (born 1996, Spain), who finished 2025 ranked fifth individually with 13,040 points alongside Claudia Fernández.

Josemaría's aggressive play and González's solid all-around game make this a pair that could challenge Triay/Brea from the very first tournament.

### 3. Ariana Sánchez and Andrea Ustero

**Ariana Sánchez** (born 1995, Spain) was the other half of the legendary Sánchez/Josemaría partnership. Ranked fourth individually in 2025 with 14,720 points, Sánchez has teamed up with **Andrea Ustero** (born 1998, Spain), who finished eighth individually with 6,625 points. Sánchez's experience and winning mentality combined with Ustero's energy and improving game creates an intriguing combination.

### 4. Sofía Araújo and Claudia Fernández

**Claudia Fernández** (born 1999, Spain), ranked fifth in 2025 alongside González, has formed a new pair with **Sofía Araújo** (born 2000, Portugal). Araújo finished 2025 ranked seventh with 7,030 points and has been steadily climbing the rankings. This Spanish-Portuguese duo brings youth, athleticism, and ambition.

### 5. Tamara Icardo and Claudia Jensen

Icardo (ranked tenth in 2025 with 6,050 points) partners with Jensen in another new pairing that reflects the wholesale reshuffling of the women's game.

## The big storylines of 2026

**Can anyone catch Coello/Tapia?** On the men's side, the gap between the top pair and the rest is enormous. Galán/Chingotto are the closest challengers, but even they trail by nearly 5,000 points.

**The Sánchez/Josemaría aftermath.** The split of women's padel's most dominant pair has created a fascinating dynamic. Both players have formed competitive new partnerships, and the question of who thrives in their new configuration will dominate the women's narrative all season.

**The Lebrón redemption arc.** Juan Lebrón was the undisputed face of men's padel for years alongside Galán. His new partnership with Augsburger represents either a return to the top or the confirmation that the game has moved past him. The stakes are high.

**Youth movement.** Players born in the 2000s — Coello, Tapia, Brea, Augsburger, Araújo — are no longer emerging talents. They are the establishment. The generational shift in padel is complete.

## How rankings work in Premier Padel

Points are awarded based on tournament category and finish position:

- **Grand Slams (P1):** up to 2,000 points per player
- **Major events:** up to 1,500 points
- **Regular tour events:** variable point allocations
- Points accumulated over a rolling 52-week period
- Both players in a pair earn equal individual ranking points

Rankings determine seedings for tournaments, entry into the highest-tier events, and a player's position in the official FIP world rankings.

On the women's side, the post-split landscape means everything is up for grabs. Either way, the depth of talent across both tours has never been greater, and the early-season results will set the tone for what could be a defining year for professional padel.
