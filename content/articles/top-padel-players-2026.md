# Top 10 Padel Players in 2026: Men's and Women's Rankings

The 2026 Premier Padel season has opened with a familiar pair at the summit but a dramatically reshuffled cast behind them. The off-season brought a wave of separations, new partnerships, and ambitious projects that have made the race for rankings more open and unpredictable than at any point in recent memory.

Here is your guide to the top players and pairs in both the men's and women's game as the 2026 season gets underway, based on the official FIP rankings and the start-of-season Premier Padel standings.

## Men's Rankings: The Top Pairs of 2026

### 1. Arturo Coello and Agustin Tapia (39,600 points)

**The undisputed number ones.** Coello and Tapia enter 2026 as the dominant force in men's padel, and it is not particularly close. The Spanish-Argentine duo accumulated 19,800 FIP ranking points each in 2025, winning 13 titles including the Premier Padel Finals. Their 47-match winning streak during the 2024 season remains one of the most remarkable records in the sport's history.

**Arturo Coello** (born 2002, Spain) is the younger half of the pair and has developed into one of the most complete players in padel. His net game is devastating -- fast hands, sharp reflexes, and an ability to close out points that puts constant pressure on opponents. At just 23, Coello is already playing at a level that took previous generations years longer to reach.

**Agustin Tapia** (born 2000, Argentina) is widely regarded as the most naturally talented player in the game. His left-handed play from the backhand side complements Coello perfectly, and his shot-making ability borders on the extraordinary. Tapia's management of high-pressure moments has matured significantly, making an already exceptional player even more complete.

Together, they have dominated three consecutive seasons (2023, 2024, 2025) and show no signs of slowing down. They opened 2026 with a commanding victory at the Riyadh P1, suggesting the chasing pack still has significant ground to cover.

### 2. Alejandro Galan and Federico Chingotto (34,640 points)

**The main challengers.** Galan and Chingotto are the only pair within realistic striking distance of Coello and Tapia. Their 2025 season produced 17,320 FIP points each, firmly establishing them as the second-best pair in the world.

**Alejandro Galan** (born 1996, Spain) is a former world number one (with Juan Lebron) who reinvented his game alongside Chingotto. Galan's power, particularly on the smash, and his court coverage make him one of the most imposing physical presences in padel.

**Federico Chingotto** (born 1996, Argentina) brings flair, creativity, and some of the best defensive skills in the sport. His ability to retrieve seemingly impossible balls and turn defense into attack is central to the pair's identity. Chingotto's theatrical style also makes him one of the most entertaining players to watch.

### 3. Franco Stupaczuk and Miguel Yanguas (14,277 points)

**The ambitious new project.** One of the most talked-about partnerships of the off-season, Stupaczuk and Yanguas combine Argentine grit with Spanish tactical intelligence.

**Franco Stupaczuk** (born 1992, Argentina), known universally as "Stupa," is a veteran who finished 2025 ranked fifth individually with 7,905 FIP points. His powerful left-handed game and years of experience at the top level bring reliability and firepower.

**Miguel Yanguas** (born 1998, Spain) finished 2025 ranked eighth individually as part of the Nieto/Yanguas pair, with 6,322 points. His technical skill and ability to construct points from the right side complement Stupa's aggressive left-handed play.

### 4. Juan Lebron and Leandro Augsburger (13,000 points)

**The explosive new duo.** Perhaps the most intriguing partnership of 2026, Lebron and Augsburger brings together a former world number one with one of the fastest-rising players of recent seasons.

**Juan Lebron** (born 1995, Spain) needs no introduction. The former world number one (alongside Ale Galan from 2020 to 2023) finished 2025 ranked sixth individually with 7,845 points. After his partnership with Galan ended and subsequent pairings did not recapture the magic, Lebron has bet on a fresh start with Augsburger.

**Leandro Augsburger** (born 2001, Argentina) finished 2025 ranked tenth individually with 5,155 points and is seen as one of the brightest young talents in padel. His aggressive style and comfort at the net could complement Lebron's all-court game beautifully -- if the chemistry clicks.

### 5. Coki Nieto and Jon Sanz (11,447 points)

A new pairing born from the off-season reshuffle. Nieto (formerly with Yanguas) and Sanz bring solid technical foundations and the hunger of players seeking to establish themselves in the top tier.

### 6. Paquito Navarro and Francisco "Fran" Guerrero (10,525 points)

**Paquito Navarro** (born 1990, Spain) is one of the most charismatic and experienced players on tour. His powerful smash and crowd-pleasing style remain formidable. Paired with the consistent Guerrero, this team has the firepower to upset any pair on a given day.

### 7. Martin Di Nenno and Alejandro "Momo" Gonzalez (9,460 points)

**Martin Di Nenno** (born 1995, Argentina) is a tactical mastermind whose cerebral approach to padel sets him apart. His partnership with Gonzalez pairs his strategic brain with a solid right-side player. Di Nenno's ability to read the game and construct points makes this pair dangerous in extended rallies.

### 8. Javi Garrido and Lucas Bergamini (7,078 points)

Rounding out the top eight, Garrido and Bergamini represent the depth of talent on the 2026 tour. Both are capable of producing top-level padel, and their consistency could see them climb further as the season progresses.

## Women's Rankings: The Top Pairs of 2026

The women's game underwent its most dramatic off-season in years, headlined by the split of Ariana Sanchez and Paula Josemaria -- one of the most successful partnerships in padel history. The ripple effects have reshaped the entire top 10.

### 1. Gemma Triay and Delfina Brea

**The undisputed world number ones.** Triay and Brea closed 2025 with 18,060 FIP points each, comfortably atop the rankings. Their consistency, tactical versatility, and ability to perform in the biggest moments have made them the benchmark pair in women's padel.

**Gemma Triay** (born 1993, Spain) is one of the most decorated players in the women's game. Her left-handed play, tactical intelligence, and fierce competitiveness define the pair's identity. Triay's decision to stay with Brea heading into 2026 -- reportedly turning down an approach from Josemaria -- underscores her commitment to the project.

**Delfina Brea** (born 2000, Argentina) has blossomed into one of the most complete players in women's padel. Her powerful groundstrokes from the right side, combined with improving net skills, make her the perfect complement to Triay's all-court game. At just 25, Brea's ceiling remains enormously high.

### 2. Beatriz Gonzalez and Paula Josemaria

The biggest new partnership of 2026. After five years of dominance alongside Ariana Sanchez, **Paula Josemaria** (born 1996, Spain) -- ranked third individually in 2025 with 14,720 points -- has formed a new pair with **Beatriz Gonzalez** (born 1996, Spain), who finished 2025 ranked fifth individually with 13,040 points alongside Claudia Fernandez.

Josemaria's aggressive play and Gonzalez's solid all-around game make this a pair that could challenge Triay and Brea from the very first tournament.

### 3. Ariana Sanchez and Andrea Ustero

**Ariana Sanchez** (born 1995, Spain) was the other half of the legendary Sanchez/Josemaria partnership. Ranked fourth individually in 2025 with 14,720 points, Sanchez has teamed up with **Andrea Ustero** (born 1998, Spain), who finished eighth individually with 6,625 points. Sanchez's experience and winning mentality combined with Ustero's energy and improving game creates an intriguing combination.

### 4. Sofia Araujo and Claudia Fernandez

**Claudia Fernandez** (born 1999, Spain), ranked fifth in 2025 alongside Gonzalez, has formed a new pair with **Sofia Araujo** (born 2000, Portugal). Araujo finished 2025 ranked seventh with 7,030 points and has been steadily climbing the rankings. This Spanish-Portuguese duo brings youth, athleticism, and ambition.

### 5. Tamara Icardo and Claudia Jensen

Icardo (ranked tenth in 2025 with 6,050 points) partners with Jensen in another new pairing that reflects the wholesale reshuffling of the women's game.

## The Big Storylines of 2026

**Can anyone catch Coello/Tapia?** On the men's side, the gap between the top pair and the rest is enormous. Galan/Chingotto are the closest challengers, but even they trail by nearly 5,000 points heading into 2026.

**The Sanchez/Josemaria aftermath.** The split of women's padel's most dominant pair has created a fascinating dynamic. Both players have formed competitive new partnerships, and the question of who thrives in their new configuration will dominate the women's narrative all season.

**The Lebron redemption arc.** Juan Lebron was the undisputed face of men's padel for years alongside Galan. His new partnership with Augsburger represents either a return to the top or the confirmation that the game has moved past him. The stakes are high.

**Youth movement.** Players born in the 2000s -- Coello, Tapia, Brea, Augsburger, Araujo -- are no longer emerging talents. They are the establishment. The generational shift in padel is complete.

## How Rankings Work in Premier Padel

The Premier Padel ranking system awards points based on tournament category and finish position:

- **Grand Slams (P1):** Up to 2,000 points per player
- **Major events:** Up to 1,500 points
- **Regular tour events:** Variable point allocations
- Points are accumulated over a rolling 52-week period
- Both players in a pair earn equal individual ranking points

Rankings determine seedings for tournaments, entry into the highest-tier events, and a player's position in the official FIP world rankings.
