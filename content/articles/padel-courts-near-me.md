# How to Find Padel Courts Near You: The Complete Guide

You have decided to try padel. Or maybe you have played before and want to find courts in a new city. Either way, the first practical question is the same: where do you actually play?

A decade ago, finding a padel court outside of Spain or Argentina was a genuine challenge. In 2026, the sport's explosive growth means courts are popping up everywhere from London to Dubai to Tokyo. But knowing how to find them, book them, and show up prepared can make the difference between a frustrating first experience and the start of an addiction.

This guide covers every method for finding padel courts, the best booking platforms, and everything you need to know before your first session.

## The Best Apps and Platforms for Finding Courts

### Playtomic

**The go-to platform for padel worldwide.**

Playtomic is the largest padel booking platform in the world, operating in over 40 countries with thousands of connected clubs. If there is a padel court near you, chances are high that Playtomic lists it.

**What Playtomic offers:**
- **Court search and booking** with real-time availability
- **Player matching** -- find partners or opponents when you do not have a group of four
- **Club profiles** with photos, reviews, court types, and pricing
- **In-app payment** so you can book and pay in seconds
- **Rankings and stats** for competitive players
- **Tournament and league listings**

**How to use it:**
1. Download the Playtomic app (iOS or Android) or visit playtomic.io
2. Set your location or search for a specific city
3. Browse available courts, filtered by date, time, and price
4. Book and pay through the app
5. Show up and play

Playtomic charges clubs a commission on bookings made through the platform. For players, the booking price shown in the app is typically the full price with no additional fees.

**Tip:** Enable notifications for your favorite clubs. Popular courts at peak times (weekday evenings, weekend mornings) book up fast, sometimes days in advance.

### Padel Manager

**Strong in Southern Europe and Latin America.**

Padel Manager is a club management system that also offers a player-facing booking interface. It is particularly popular in Spain and Portugal.

**What it offers:**
- Court booking for clubs that use the Padel Manager system
- Class and coaching session bookings
- Club membership management
- Tournament registration

Padel Manager is less of a discovery platform than Playtomic -- it works best when you already know which club you want to book at and they use the system.

### Matchi

**The Nordic specialist.**

Matchi is widely used in Sweden, Finland, Norway, and Denmark, where padel has experienced explosive growth. If you are looking for courts in Scandinavia, Matchi is often the primary booking platform.

### Other Regional Platforms

Depending on your location, you may find local booking platforms that serve your area:

- **Racketpal** (UK and Ireland)
- **Padeltrack** (various European markets)
- **Individual club websites and apps** (many larger clubs have their own booking systems)

## Using Google Maps

Google Maps is a surprisingly effective tool for finding padel courts, especially in areas where dedicated padel apps have limited coverage.

### How to Search

1. Open Google Maps (app or web)
2. Search for **"padel court"**, **"padel club"**, or **"padel near me"**
3. Scan the results for locations, ratings, and reviews
4. Tap on a result to see photos, hours, contact information, and reviews

### Tips for Better Results

- **Try multiple search terms.** Some facilities list themselves as "padel center," "padel club," or even "sports center with padel." Try variations if your first search does not return results.
- **Check reviews.** Google reviews from other players often mention court quality, surface type, lighting, and availability -- information that official listings may not provide.
- **Look at photos.** User-uploaded photos give you a realistic picture of the facility. Check for the condition of the glass walls, surface quality, and overall cleanliness.
- **Verify hours.** Some courts have limited hours or seasonal closures. Call ahead or check the website if you are visiting for the first time.
- **Use satellite view.** You can often spot padel courts in satellite imagery -- they are distinctive rectangular enclosed structures, sometimes with blue or green surfaces visible from above.

## Club Directories and Federations

### National Federation Websites

Most national padel or tennis federations maintain directories of registered courts and clubs:

- **Spain:** Real Federacion Espanola de Padel (FEP)
- **Italy:** Federazione Italiana Tennis e Padel (FITP)
- **France:** Federation Francaise de Tennis (FFT) -- padel falls under the tennis federation
- **UK:** Lawn Tennis Association (LTA) -- includes a padel court finder
- **Sweden:** Svenska Padelforbundet
- **Germany:** Deutscher Padel Bund

These directories are often the most comprehensive listings for their respective countries, including courts that may not appear on commercial booking platforms.

### International Padel Federation (FIP)

The FIP website (padelfip.com) provides links to national federations worldwide, which can direct you to local court directories.

## Types of Courts You Will Encounter

Not all padel courts are created equal. Here is what you might find:

### Indoor Courts
- Climate-controlled environments
- Consistent playing conditions year-round
- Often higher booking rates
- Common in Northern Europe, the UK, and regions with variable weather
- Typically artificial grass or carpet surfaces

### Outdoor Courts
- Natural lighting during daytime play
- May have floodlights for evening sessions
- Subject to weather conditions (rain, wind, extreme heat)
- Generally lower booking rates than indoor
- Common in Spain, Portugal, Southern France, and the Middle East

### Glass Courts vs. Concrete Wall Courts
- **Glass courts** provide the standard playing experience with predictable ball rebounds and spectator visibility
- **Concrete or brick wall courts** are cheaper to build and maintain but offer a different (faster) rebound
- Professional and most modern facilities use glass

### Surface Types
- **Artificial grass with sand infill** (blue or green) -- the standard
- **Concrete** -- found in some older or budget facilities
- **Carpet** -- some indoor facilities

### Court Quality Indicators
When visiting a new court, look for:
- **Even, well-maintained surface** (no patches, tears, or uneven sand distribution)
- **Clean glass** (dirty glass affects ball rebound and visibility)
- **Proper lighting** (no dark spots or glare)
- **Intact mesh fencing** (no holes or sagging)
- **Functional net** (correct height, no sagging beyond regulation)
- **Good drainage** for outdoor courts (standing water after rain is a red flag)

## Booking Tips

### When to Book

**Peak times** (highest demand, highest prices):
- Weekday evenings (6:00 PM - 10:00 PM)
- Saturday and Sunday mornings (9:00 AM - 1:00 PM)

**Off-peak times** (easier to book, often cheaper):
- Weekday mornings and early afternoons
- Sunday evenings
- Summer months in hot climates (everyone plays early morning or late evening)

### How Far in Advance

- **Popular urban courts:** Book 2-7 days in advance for peak times
- **Suburban or less busy clubs:** Same-day booking often available
- **League and tournament bookings:** May lock up courts weeks in advance

### Pricing

Booking rates vary enormously by location:
- **Spain:** 10-30 EUR per 90-minute session (4 players split the cost)
- **UK and Northern Europe:** 30-70 EUR per session
- **UAE and Middle East:** 30-80 EUR per session
- **US and Canada:** 40-100 USD per session
- **Argentina:** Significantly cheaper than European rates

Remember that the cost is split among four players. A 60 EUR booking is only 15 EUR per person for 90 minutes of exercise and social time -- competitive with most fitness activities.

### Cancellation Policies

Most platforms and clubs have cancellation policies:
- **Free cancellation** up to 12-24 hours before the booking
- **Partial refund** for cancellations within 12 hours
- **No refund** for no-shows
- Check the specific policy before booking, especially for expensive courts

## What to Expect at Your First Session

### What to Bring

- **A padel racket.** If you do not own one, most clubs rent rackets for a small fee (typically 3-10 EUR). Some clubs include racket rental in the court price.
- **Padel or tennis shoes.** Proper court shoes with non-marking soles are essential. Running shoes do not provide adequate lateral support.
- **Comfortable sportswear.** Standard athletic clothing is fine. No specific dress code at most recreational facilities.
- **Water.** Courts can get hot, especially indoor facilities and outdoor courts in warm climates.
- **Balls.** Some clubs provide balls with the court booking. Others require you to buy a can at reception. A can of three padel balls typically costs 4-8 EUR.

### What Happens on Court

If you are a complete beginner:

1. **The serve is underhand.** Bounce the ball and hit it below waist height. Serve diagonally into the opposite service box.
2. **The walls are in play.** After the ball bounces on your side, it can hit the glass walls and you can still play it. This is the most distinctive feature of padel.
3. **You play doubles.** Padel is always 4 players (2 vs 2). One player covers the left side, the other the right.
4. **The scoring is like tennis.** 15, 30, 40, game. First to 6 games wins the set. Most social matches play one or two sets.
5. **Positioning matters more than power.** Padel rewards placement, patience, and teamwork over brute force. The best position is at the net.

### Etiquette

- **Arrive on time.** Court bookings are fixed time slots, and showing up late cuts into your playing time.
- **Warm up briefly.** A few minutes of gentle hitting before playing points.
- **Communicate with your partner.** Call "mine" or "yours" to avoid confusion.
- **Retrieve balls that enter neighboring courts** at a natural break in play. Never walk onto an adjacent court during a point.
- **Leave the court clean.** Pick up ball cans, water bottles, and any trash.
- **Respect noise levels.** Excessive shouting or racket throwing is frowned upon everywhere.

## Finding Players: What If You Do Not Have Four?

Padel requires four players, which can be a barrier if you are new to the sport or new to an area. Here is how to find playing partners:

### Open Play and Americano Events
Many clubs organize **open play sessions** or **Americano tournaments** where individual players sign up and are randomly paired. These are the best way to meet other players and integrate into the local padel community.

### Playtomic Player Matching
Playtomic's match feature lets you create an "open match" where other players can join to fill out a foursome. You set the level, time, and court, and the app does the rest.

### Social Media Groups
Facebook groups, WhatsApp groups, and local community boards often have active padel communities:
- Search for "[Your City] padel" on Facebook
- Ask at your local club's reception about WhatsApp groups
- Check Reddit (r/padel) or local sports forums

### Club Leagues and Programs
Most clubs run weekly leagues, ladders, or group coaching sessions. These are structured ways to play regularly, meet other players, and improve your game.

## Country-Specific Tips

### Spain
Courts are abundant but can be very busy at peak times. Book ahead for evening slots. Many municipal sports centers offer courts at subsidized rates.

### United Kingdom
The number of courts has grown dramatically but remains limited compared to demand. Book well in advance. Indoor courts are standard due to the climate.

### United States
Padel is still emerging. Courts are concentrated in major cities (Miami, New York, Los Angeles, Houston, Dallas). Expect to pay premium rates. Some tennis clubs are converting courts.

### UAE and Middle East
Indoor facilities dominate due to the heat. Excellent quality courts, often as part of larger sports or leisure complexes. Prices are moderate to high.

### Scandinavia
Indoor courts are standard. Matchi is the primary booking platform in most Nordic countries. The padel community is large and enthusiastic.

## Wrapping Up

Finding a padel court in 2026 is easier than it has ever been. Between Playtomic, Google Maps, national federation directories, and the growing number of courts worldwide, the infrastructure is there. The harder part, frankly, is stopping once you start.

Download a booking app, find your nearest court, grab three friends (or let the app find them for you), and get on court. The padel community is one of the most welcoming in sport, and the barrier to entry has never been lower.
