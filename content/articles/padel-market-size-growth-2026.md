# The Padel Economy in 2026: Market Size, Growth Rate, and Where the Money Is

Padel has crossed the threshold from niche racquet sport to legitimate global industry. The numbers released over the past twelve months tell a story that few sports can match: double-digit growth in courts, clubs, and players, backed by institutional capital and blue-chip sponsorships. For entrepreneurs scouting the next big opportunity in sports and leisure, and for investors sizing up a market still in its early innings, here is a comprehensive breakdown of where padel stands financially in 2026.

## Global Market Size

The padel market remains difficult to pin down with a single number, because the ecosystem spans court construction, equipment manufacturing, media rights, facility operations, technology platforms, coaching, and events. Various research estimates place the global padel market between $300 million and $500 million in direct market value as of 2025, depending on what is included in the calculation. One widely cited figure from Padel United Sports Club, referencing industry data, valued the padel market at $204 million in 2023 with projections reaching nearly $493 million by 2032 (Padel United Sports Club, 2025). A more bullish estimate from Deloitte, cited by Conquer Padel in its investor materials, projects the broader padel economy could reach $6 billion by 2028 when factoring in facility construction, real estate, hospitality, and adjacent revenues (Deloitte, referenced by BusinessWire, 2025).

What is not in dispute is the trajectory. Every major data source points to compound annual growth rates between 10% and 20%, depending on the segment. The equipment market alone -- rackets, balls, shoes, apparel -- is growing faster than any comparable category in racquet sports.

## Courts and Infrastructure: The Hard Numbers

The most concrete measure of padel's expansion is physical infrastructure. According to the FIP World Padel Report 2025, published in December 2025, global padel courts reached 77,300 -- a 15.2% increase over the prior year, with 14,355 new courts built in 2025 alone. The number of padel clubs worldwide surpassed 24,600, up 16.1% year-over-year (FIP World Padel Report 2025).

The sport is now played across 150 countries and 20 dependent territories. Industry projections consistently point to 85,000-90,000 courts by the end of 2026, and potentially crossing 100,000 by 2027-2028.

### Courts by Country (2025 estimates)

- **Spain**: ~17,000 courts -- the undisputed global capital of padel. For context, Spain has roughly 5,700 tennis courts. Padel courts outnumber tennis courts 3-to-1.
- **Italy**: ~9,700 courts -- the fastest-growing major European market, having roughly doubled its court count since 2021.
- **Argentina**: ~7,000 courts -- padel's spiritual homeland, where the sport was invented.
- **France**: ~4,000 courts -- a 55% increase in the last 18 months alone (Tennis Creative, January 2026).
- **Sweden**: ~3,000+ courts -- one of the highest per-capita ratios in the world.
- **United Kingdom**: 1,000+ courts -- surpassing the milestone in 2025 after explosive growth.
- **United States**: ~650+ courts as of early 2025 (FIP data), with forecasts of up to 15,000 by 2029 (Conquer Padel, BusinessWire, 2025).

The Playtomic Global Padel Report 2025, prepared in collaboration with Strategy& (PwC's strategic consulting arm), documented more than 7,100 new courts and 3,280 new clubs opened worldwide during 2024 alone. Padel clubs increased 22% globally in 2024, with particularly strong growth in Southern Europe (Sports Destination Management, 2025).

## Player Population

The FIP World Padel Report 2025 estimates over 35 million active padel players globally, up from roughly 25-30 million in 2023. Europe accounts for approximately 61% of the global player base. Women represent 40% of all padel players -- a notably higher female participation rate than most racquet sports (Sporting Goods Intelligence, December 2025).

Spain leads with an estimated 8-9 million players, making padel the second most popular participation sport in the country behind football. Argentina, Italy, and Sweden round out the top tier. In the U.S., participation passed 100,000 by early 2025 (SGB Media Online), a small number in absolute terms but growing at triple-digit rates.

Perhaps the most telling statistic: the FIP reported a 42% growth in members registered with national federations compared to 2024. This suggests the sport is not just attracting casual participants but converting them into committed, organized players.

## Revenue Breakdown

The padel economy generates revenue across several distinct categories:

### Court Construction and Equipment
Court construction remains the largest single segment. With thousands of new courts being built annually at costs ranging from $25,000 to $150,000 per court (depending on indoor/outdoor configuration and market), the global court construction market alone likely exceeds $500 million annually. Equipment -- rackets, balls, shoes, bags, and apparel -- represents a rapidly growing segment, with brands like Bullpadel, Head, Wilson, Adidas (via All For Padel), and Nox competing aggressively for market share.

### Facility Operations
Court rental revenue is the bread and butter of the padel economy. With 77,000+ courts worldwide and average hourly rental rates of EUR 20-60 depending on market and time of day, aggregate court rental revenue likely runs into the billions annually. A well-run club in a mature market can generate EUR 40,000-70,000 per court per year in rental revenue alone.

### Media Rights and Broadcasting
Premier Padel, the sport's unified professional tour (officially the Qatar Airways Premier Padel Tour), has secured broadcast distribution across more than 190 territories. beIN Media Group in February 2026 renewed its rights deal covering 39 territories across MENA, APAC, and North America. Red Bull TV provides free global streaming coverage. While specific rights fees have not been publicly disclosed, the progression from zero meaningful media revenue in 2021 to multi-territory deals with major broadcasters represents significant momentum.

### Sponsorships
The Qatar Airways title sponsorship of the Premier Padel Tour, announced in 2024 as a multi-year deal, marked a step change in padel's commercial credibility. Additional tour-level sponsors include Red Bull, Wilson, Bullpadel, Playtomic, MejorSet, Betsson, and NTT Data (Sportcal, May 2025). CUPRA sponsors the FIP Tour. Individual tournaments attract regional sponsors, and the hospitality opportunities at padel events -- with their intimate, social atmosphere -- command premium pricing relative to audience size.

## Investment Landscape

Capital is flowing into padel from multiple directions:

### Institutional and Strategic Investment
- **Qatar Sports Investments (QSI)**, the entity behind Paris Saint-Germain, acquired the World Padel Tour in 2023 and unified it with Premier Padel under the FIP umbrella. QSI is chaired by Nasser Al-Khelaifi, who also chairs the Premier Padel board.
- **MCH Private Equity** invested in All For Padel (an Adidas-licensed equipment brand) through its Spain Oman Private Equity Fund (PitchBook, 2024).
- **Playtomic**, the dominant booking and club management platform, raised $70 million in equity and debt in March 2025, led by Match Invest. The platform reported EUR 346 million transacted (+51% year-over-year) and EUR 29 million in net revenue (+38% YoY) as of September 2025 (Crowdcube, November 2025). Rafael Nadal serves as brand ambassador.
- **VVV Sports**, a publicly listed sports and entertainment vehicle on the UK's Aquis Exchange, acquired padel-focused R3 Sport in November 2025 (Padel Business Magazine, February 2026).

### U.S.-Focused Capital
- **Pro Padel** raised $10 million in April 2025, as reported by Forbes. The round drew celebrity investors from entertainment, soccer, and Formula 1.
- **Epic Padel** secured a $10 million seed round backed by Off Court Ventures, Silverback Capital Group, and angel investors (Wamda, September 2025).
- **EEP Capital**, a venture capital firm focused on padel backed by Swedish NHL players Filip Forsberg, Jacob Markstrom, Mattias Ekholm, Elias Lindholm, and William Nylander, was among the first dedicated funds to invest in U.S. padel infrastructure (Front Office Sports, July 2025).
- **Conquer Padel** announced a $15 million private equity offering for U.S. expansion, with a franchise model targeting 50 locations (BusinessWire, March 2025).

### Celebrity and Athlete Investors
Beyond the NHL contingent at EEP Capital, padel has attracted investment from professional athletes in football, rugby, and motorsport. Scottish rugby players invested in Smash Padel, a UK-based operator (padel1969.com, 2025). The social nature of the sport and its association with European and Latin American celebrity culture continue to draw high-profile backers.

## Comparison to Tennis and Pickleball

Understanding padel's market position requires context from its closest competitors.

**Tennis** remains the giant of racquet sports. Market research estimates place the global tennis industry at $5.6-9.3 billion in 2025, depending on scope, growing at a CAGR of roughly 6-7% (Market Research Future; Business Research Insights; Research and Markets). Tennis has approximately 87 million players worldwide and deeply established media rights generating hundreds of millions annually.

**Pickleball** is padel's most direct comparable in terms of growth narrative. The global pickleball market was valued at approximately $1.8-2.2 billion in 2024-2025, with a CAGR projected at 10-15% through 2034 (Market.us; Maximize Market Research). Pickleball had nearly 20 million U.S. participants in 2024 (Sports and Fitness Industry Association) and over 70,000 courts in the U.S. as of 2025. However, pickleball remains overwhelmingly a North American phenomenon, whereas padel's player base is distributed across Europe, Latin America, and increasingly the Middle East and Asia.

The key distinction: padel's infrastructure costs are higher (enclosed glass courts vs. pickleball's simpler setup), but this creates a moat for established operators and higher revenue per court hour. Padel's facility model also naturally lends itself to premium hospitality and food-and-beverage integration in ways that open-air pickleball courts do not.

## Premier Padel: The Tour Economy

The 2026 Qatar Airways Premier Padel Tour features approximately 21 events across a P2, P1, and Major hierarchy, plus the season-ending Finals. Tournaments are spread across five continents, with new stops in the United States (Miami), Middle East (Riyadh, Doha), and traditional European strongholds (Rome, Paris, Barcelona).

Prize money per tournament tier in 2026:
- **Majors**: EUR 500,000 total prize pool per category (men's and women's)
- **P1 events**: EUR 260,000 per category
- **P2 events**: ~EUR 147,500-265,000 per category

The combined season prize pool across all Premier Padel events runs into the millions of euros -- a dramatic increase from the sport's pre-unification era, though still a fraction of professional tennis purses.

Broadcast distribution now spans 190+ territories, with beIN Sports covering MENA, Turkey, APAC, and North America, and Red Bull TV providing global free-to-air streaming. Nearly 75% of 2026 events are expected to be played indoors, improving production quality and broadcast consistency (World Padel Network, February 2026).

## Growth Drivers

**Accessibility**: Padel's smaller court, enclosed walls, and doubles format make it easier to learn and more social than tennis. New players can enjoy competitive rallies within their first session.

**Social dynamics**: The doubles-only format and intimate court setting create a social experience that translates into higher retention and community building -- critical factors for club operators.

**Urbanization**: Padel courts require less space than tennis courts (200 sqm vs. 650+ sqm), making them viable in dense urban environments where real estate is at a premium.

**Olympic aspirations**: While padel is not yet on the Olympic program, its inclusion is widely discussed and would represent a transformational catalyst for global awareness and government funding.

**Technology platforms**: Playtomic and similar platforms have dramatically reduced friction in court booking, matchmaking, and community building, accelerating adoption curves in new markets.

## Potential Headwinds

**Oversupply risk**: In mature markets like Spain, some regions may be approaching court saturation, and newer clubs are competing for a finite pool of peak-hour demand.

**Construction costs**: Building padel courts is capital-intensive compared to pickleball or multi-use sports facilities. Indoor courts in northern climates can cost EUR 100,000-150,000 per court, raising breakeven timelines.

**Weather dependency**: Outdoor-only facilities in seasonal climates face significant utilization challenges during winter months, limiting annual revenue potential.

**Competition from pickleball**: In the U.S. market specifically, pickleball has a significant head start in facilities, organized play, and cultural awareness. Padel must carve out a differentiated position rather than competing head-to-head.

**Media rights maturation**: While broadcast distribution has expanded rapidly, padel has not yet proven it can command the per-event rights fees that would sustain a fully professional athlete ecosystem comparable to tennis.

**Regulatory fragmentation**: The sport's governance structure -- spanning the FIP, Premier Padel, national federations, and various commercial interests -- has historically been complex. The 2023 unification resolved the WPT/Premier Padel split, but ongoing alignment among stakeholders remains important.

## The Bottom Line

Padel in 2026 is a sport that has graduated from "fastest-growing" curiosity to investable industry. With 77,000+ courts, 35 million+ players, institutional backing from QSI and major broadcasters, and a capital pipeline flowing from venture funds, private equity, and franchise operators, the structural foundations are in place for continued expansion.

The most compelling opportunities lie in underserved markets -- the U.S., UK, France, Germany, Asia, and the Middle East -- where court supply still lags demand. For operators and investors, the question is no longer whether padel will grow, but how to capture value in a market that is maturing faster than most predicted.

The numbers suggest a sport on the cusp of its next inflection point. Whether padel reaches the scale of tennis or settles into a prosperous middle tier between pickleball and tennis, the economic opportunity in 2026 is real and measurable.
